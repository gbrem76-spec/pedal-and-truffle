The Experience

Adventure Made Effortless

Most people will never ride the Attack of the Buns route. We've changed that.

The Attack of the Buns is a 320km bikepacking legend — celebrated for its breathtaking Southern Tablelands scenery, remote wilderness, and unforgettable gravel roads. Designed for serious bikepackers, it demands days of hard effort and expert navigation.

Pedal & Truffle takes the magic of this iconic route and opens it to everyone. With premium electric bikes, a detailed GPS route loaded and ready to go, and a curated overnight stay at the extraordinary Charley's Forest Truffles, this is adventure travel at its finest — without the suffering.

Your journey begins at Bungendore train station — accessible from Sydney or Canberra — and finishes at the South Coast in Nowra, with every detail handled for you.

The Journey

Bungendore to Nowra

Two days of riding through forests, tablelands, and wilderness — with a world-class overnight stop at the heart of it all.

🚂
01
Bungendore
Day 1 · Start

Collect your electric bike and GPS-loaded route at the historic Bungendore train station. Fuel up at The Gathering café before heading into the tablelands.

🥐
02
Braidwood
Day 1 · ~60km

Ride through sweeping gravel roads and ancient eucalypt forest to the heritage town of Braidwood. Stop at the legendary Dojo Bakery — the best artisan bread in NSW.

🍄
03
Mongarlowe
Day 1 · Evening

Arrive at Charley's Forest Truffles for your overnight stay. Boutique accommodation on a working truffle farm — where the pace slows and the evening is yours to savour.

🌊
04
Nowra
Day 2 · Finish

A final day's ride through the Budawang wilderness and Morton National Park, arriving at Nowra on the South Coast — a well-earned finish to an extraordinary journey.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Do I need cycling experience? +
Not at all. Our premium electric bikes make the hills effortless. If you can ride a bike, you can ride this tour. We cater to all fitness levels — ride at your own pace, stop whenever you like. No lycra required.
How do I get to the start? +
Bungendore is on the Canberra train line, with regular services from Sydney and Canberra. We can also arrange transfers from Canberra CBD. Full logistics briefing is provided upon booking.
What's the terrain like? +
A mix of sealed country roads and light gravel tracks — beautiful tablelands scenery throughout. The electric bikes handle all the hills with ease. Total riding is approximately 160km spread over two days, with plenty of stops along the way.
What's included in the overnight stay? +
Your night at Charley's Forest Truffles includes private accommodation, secure bike storage, a truffle-focused dinner, and breakfast the next morning. It's a working truffle farm with extraordinary produce — the overnight stop is genuinely one of the tour highlights.
What's the group size? +
Classic tours run with a maximum of 8 riders for a personal, uncrowded experience. Private tours are exclusive — just your group, your GPS, and the open road. We recommend booking early as spots fill quickly.
What happens if the weather is bad? +
We monitor conditions carefully and communicate with all guests in the week before departure. Wet weather gear is available. In the event of severe conditions, we'll work with you to reschedule at no cost. Australian country weather is generally very rider-friendly.
